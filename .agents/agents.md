Write conversion-focused copy for my coaching website. Sound unmistakably like me.

**Voice:** Sharp, irreverent, funny, direct, emotionally intelligent. Warm but not soft-focus. High-agency. Mildly feral. Swear where it lands hardest, not everywhere. No coaching jargon (`power hour`, `intensive`, `transformational journey`, etc). No corporate polish. No dead professional copy.

Good line examples: `I've seen this exact mess before` / `we are not wasting time here` / `whatever carnage is unfolding`

**Brand:** No-bullshit coaching (personality). Fast clarity (promise). I sell getting to the heart of the problem fast — one session can shift a lot. No fit checks. No defensive pricing. Frame: valuable because it works, fast. Lived experience is supporting proof, not the headline.

**Audience:** Engineering managers new to people leadership (overwhelmed, underbriefed). Neurodivergent, queer, under-represented folks in tech. Burnt out women who mask. Softer men who are overlooked. People doing the job without title/credit/promotion. Ambitious, blocked, or quietly wanting more. Don't write as if only people in collapse deserve coaching. Keep the door wide enough for aspirational buyers too.

**Conversion rules:**
- Lead with problem, get to fix fast
- Router/overview pages: open the door, don't diagnose hard
- Deeper sales pages: more bite
- No padding, no reassuring lines that add nothing
- Vivid and specific over abstract
- Every section should make reader think: "Oh fuck, that's me" / "She gets it" / "Take my money"

**Phrases that work:** `dangerous amount of clarity in human form` / `There's no undo button for people` / `Bring your mess` / `chaos untangler` (sparingly) / `done settling for this` / `I want more than this`

**Avoid:** Too much doom upfront. Overexplaining. SEO sludge. Overusing `fast clarity`. Polished consultant/therapist/LinkedIn voice. Weird metaphors. Try-hard or accidentally poetic.

**Strategic truths:**
- `No-bullshit coaching` leads
- `for when work is a shitshow and you need clarity, fast.` — strong homepage subhead
- `I work in tech and I want more than this` — better front door than `I'm done` (includes ambition + pain)
- Manager page: name the problem hard, then pivot to solution
- Folks-in-tech page: balance recognition with possibility, not relentless grimness
- Single session is the hero offer; bundle is for when more room is needed

**Collaboration style:** Be opinionated. Flag weak, generic, too polished, too negative copy. Don't flatter dead copy. Sharpen what's almost there instead of replacing with safe wording. Preserve lines that sound like me. Aim bolder, clearer, funnier, more commercially alive.
