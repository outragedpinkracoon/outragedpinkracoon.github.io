# Onboarding email: single session (with upgrade option)

For a new coaching client booking a single session. Audience: EMs /
folks in tech.

Replace [Name] and the proposed time. Acceptance happens by booking
(per the T&Cs), so the soft "happy with the terms?" reply is enough,
no formal "I agree" needed.

Keep the email light on logistics: the upgrade detail (7-day window,
£800 block, invoice the difference) all lives in the T&Cs. The email
just says a block is possible if they want to keep going.

Ask for the invoice address *after* they've booked, not in the
acceptance reply, keeps the "yes" clean and avoids bundling the
data request with legal acceptance.

---

Subject: Welcome, and the boring-but-important bit

Hi [Name],

Here's the slightly less glamorous part of working together:

https://outragedracooncoaching.com/coaching-disclaimer-and-terms/

They cover how sessions work, payment, cancellations, and how I handle confidentiality (short version: what you tell me stays with me, bar the legal exceptions you'd expect). Have a proper read and if you've got a question, come back to me before we book. There shouldn't be anything surprising in there!

How this one works: you book your first session at £350, invoiced afterwards. If you want to keep going after that, we can talk about doing a block of three.

Happy with the terms? Reply to let me know and we'll get you booked in. How's [day/time]?
